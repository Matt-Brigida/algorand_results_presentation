Crypto Momentum Factor Construction

To calculate the momentum factor for week $t$ we calculate the cumulative return for each cryptocurrency over weeks $t-5$ to $t-1$. We then create a long-short portfolio which buys the currencies in the top 25% of cumulative return, and sells the currencies in the bottom 25%. The return on this portfolio over week $t$ is our crypto momentum factor for week $t$.

Crypto SMB Factor Construction

To construct the SMB factor for week $t$ we sort all cryptocurrencies by size in week $t-1$. We then create a long-short portfolio which buys the top 25% of coins by size and sells the bottom 25%. The return on this portfolio over week $t$, less the risk free rate, is the factor realization for week $t$.

TVL Factor Construction

To construct the TVL factor we first scale TVL by market cap to a proportion of market cap which is locked. Then for each week $t$ we rank assets by TVL / Market Cap in week $t-1$. We then create a long-short portfolio which buys the top 25% of coins by TVL and sells the bottom 25%. The return on this portfolio over week $t$, less the risk free rate, is the factor realization for week $t$.

Risk Premia: Fama-MacBeth (1973)

Estimates the cross-sectional relationship between cryptocurrency expected returns and asset characteristics and risk factors.

Over time variation is only used to construct standard errors p-values

Risk Premium: Consider the CAPM

In the CAPM:

$$R_S = r_f + \beta_S (E(R_M) - r_f)$$

the Market Risk Premium is:

$$E(R_M) - r_f$$

Each $\lambda$ measures the factor's risk premium.

The $\alpha$ measures the model's pricing error.

Summary of Fama MacBeth Results

  • Insignificant alpha
  • Crypto Market Risk Premium is positive and significant, and estimated to be 10.14% per year
  • Tech and Finance related stock sectors have a positive risk premia
  • Altcoin Index measures priced risk
  • $SMB_C$ has a negative and significant risk premium
  • Inconclusive evidence with respect to TVL

Fama-Macbeth Method Description

In the first step we estimate the following time-series regression for all cryptocurrencies /i/ in our sample:

$$r_{i,t} = \beta_{0,i} + \beta_{1,i} R_{S,t} + \beta_{2,i} SMB_t + \beta_{3,i} HML_t + \xi_t$$

From this we extract the $\beta$s

In the second step we estimate the following cross-sectional regression /for each time point/ $t$ in our sample.

$$r_{i,t} = \alpha_t + \lambda_{1,t} \beta_{1,i} + \lambda_{2,t} \beta_{2,i} + \lambda_{3,t} \beta_{3,i} + \epsilon_t$$

We then take:

$$\hat{\lambda_i} = \frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=1}^T \hat{\lambda_{i,t}}$$

for factor $i=1,2,3$ and similarly:

$$\hat{\alpha} = \frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=1}^T \hat{\alpha_{t}}$$

Risk Premia: Giglio and Xiu (2021)

Fama MacBeth vs Giglio Xiu

Giglio and Xiu includes latent factors, however the method requires a balanced panel. This narrows the number of currencies and time periods, and we cannot have failed currencies. This increases survivorship bias.

Fama MacBeth can be used on an unbalanced panel, enabling a wider and longer sample, which may include dead currencies.

Fama French 1996 Analysis

Testing whether a given set of factors explain a portfolio's returns over time

$$R_{TVL} = \alpha + \beta R_C + \epsilon$$

  • where $R_{TVL}$ are crypto portfolios by TVL
  • the explanatory variables are factor portfolios (here a 1 factor model)
  • test for significant $\alpha$

Results: $\frac{TVL}{Market Cap}$

Summary of Fama French 1996 TVL Results

The one-factor crypto market returns model explains a substantial fraction of the variation in TVL (both scaled by market cap and in levels).

All alpha coefficients are insignificant.
